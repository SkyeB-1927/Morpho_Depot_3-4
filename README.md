### Thesis Work
My thesis evaluated a shared mechanism of viral inhibition. I discovered through my work that three evolutionarily distant viruses (SARS, HSV, and VSV) all utilize a shared methionine in order to attach to and inhibit mRNA export. These viruses accomplish this by using one of their protein to dock on Rae1 and Nup98 where the mRNA moleucles dock. This prevents the mRNA molecules from being exported out of the nucleus and into the cytoplasm. This is beneficial for the viruses as these viruses are able to replicate without the Rae1-Nup98 mRNA export pathway thus shutting down the host cells immune response while supporting their own proliferation (**[My Paper](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41000818/)**).

This work was made possible using comparative molecular dynamics simulations through a program called **AtomDance** (**[See here](https://github.com/gbabbitt/ATOMDANCE-comparative-protein-dynamics)**).
